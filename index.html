SeamSense

Real-time Quality Monitoring System for the Apparel Industry

In collaboration with MAS Linea Aqua Hanwella, Sri Lanka

About the System

SeamSense is a state-of-the-art real-time quality monitoring system designed for the apparel industry. It uses advanced machine learning (ML) and sensor fusion to ensure high-quality garment production. Embedded in production machinery, SeamSense continuously monitors and analyzes seam quality.
Key components include a high-precision sensor array that tracks thread tension, needle position, and fabric movement, with an ML algorithm detecting defects like skipped stitches, uneven seams, and fabric puckering. This enables immediate corrective actions and provides operators with real-time feedback and detailed reports.
SeamSense integrates easily into production lines, minimizing disruption while reducing waste, lowering costs, and improving quality. It also features predictive maintenance, analyzing trends to prevent machinery failures and reduce downtime. By enhancing precision and reliability, SeamSense sets new standards for garment manufacturing quality control.
Overall, SeamSense is a revolutionary solution that brings precision and reliability to garment manufacturing, setting new standards for quality control in the industry.

Seam Defect Detection

Our SeamSense system employs advanced Machine Learning models, specifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), to accurately detect and categorize seam defects in real-time. This ensures high-quality control measures during the manufacturing process, identifying issues such as skipped stitches, uneven seams, and fabric puckering, allowing for immediate corrective actions.

Fog Computing Integration

SeamSense integrates fog computing to efficiently process multimedia content at the network edge before transmitting it to the cloud. This approach optimizes storage and minimizes overhead while ensuring secure data transfer between the fog and cloud layers, enhancing the overall efficiency of defect detection.

User-Friendly Interface

The SeamSense system features a user-friendly interface that provides operators with real-time feedback and detailed reports on seam quality. Designed for easy integration into existing production lines, it minimizes disruption and maximizes efficiency. By ensuring consistent quality, SeamSense helps manufacturers reduce waste, lower costs, and enhance customer satisfaction.

Predictive Maintenance

In addition to real-time monitoring, SeamSense offers predictive maintenance features. The system analyzes trends to predict potential machinery failures before they occur, enabling proactive maintenance and reducing downtime. This not only ensures smooth production but also extends the lifespan of the machinery, contributing to the overall efficiency of the manufacturing process.

LITERATURE SURVEY

The apparel industry, a key player in global manufacturing, faces significant challenges in maintaining high-quality standards while keeping up with fast-paced production cycles. Traditional quality control methods, primarily manual inspections, are labor-intensive, time-consuming, and prone to human error, leading to inconsistencies in defect detection and overall quality management. These shortcomings have spurred the adoption of automated systems leveraging machine learning (ML) and computer vision techniques for real-time defect detection.

Recent advancements in machine vision, powered by Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), have been instrumental in improving the accuracy and speed of defect detection. CNNs have been widely used in the garment industry to identify various defects such as holes, stains, and fabric misalignments. However, while CNNs have demonstrated high accuracy, their application in seam defect detection requires further exploration. To bridge this gap, SeamSense utilizes the "You Only Look Once" (YOLO) family of algorithms, particularly YOLOv8 and YOLOv9, known for their real-time object detection capabilities and high precision in identifying small, complex defects.

The introduction of YOLO models marks a significant improvement over traditional defect detection methods. Redmon et al. (2016) introduced YOLO as a fast and efficient algorithm that performs object detection in a single pass over the image. In subsequent versions, such as YOLOv8 and YOLOv9, enhancements were made to improve feature extraction and detection of smaller objects, making them ideal for the apparel industry’s need to detect subtle seam defects. Research by Terven and Cordova-Esparza (2023) provides a detailed review of YOLO architectures, showcasing their potential in real-time industrial applications like defect detection in manufacturing.

One of the primary components of SeamSense is its adaptive frame extraction technique, which isolates relevant frames from video feeds, ensuring that only the necessary data is processed. This technique is critical in reducing the computational load while improving detection accuracy. Studies on adaptive frame extraction methods, such as those by Hu et al. (2024), emphasize the importance of filtering non-essential data, particularly in high-speed production environments where real-time processing is essential.

Another major technological advancement SeamSense utilizes is fog computing, which processes data closer to the source—on the production floor—rather than relying solely on cloud computing. Fog computing reduces latency, a critical factor in real-time applications, by performing initial image processing, such as content filtering and image augmentation, locally. Research on fog computing in industrial settings, such as Linthicum’s (2024) work, highlights the benefits of edge computing for reducing bandwidth and processing delays. By applying techniques such as grayscale conversion and noise reduction at the fog layer, SeamSense ensures that only high-quality data is transmitted to the cloud for further analysis.

To improve prediction accuracy and enhance decision-making, SeamSense incorporates a fusion model that combines real-time detection with historical defect data and worker-centric factors like skill levels and demographics. Time-series analysis plays a crucial role in predicting future defect trends, helping manufacturers identify patterns and plan proactive quality control interventions. The integration of time-series analysis with machine learning models has been explored in numerous studies, including Wu et al. (2023), which highlight the effectiveness of using historical data to forecast future defects in production environments.

The literature also emphasizes the importance of integrating human factors into defect prediction models. Linthicum (2023) underscores the significance of incorporating worker demographics and experience to provide more comprehensive insights into defect trends. SeamSense addresses this by incorporating worker-centric data into its fusion model, allowing the system to adapt to the specific production environment at MAS Linea Aqua.

In summary, the SeamSense system builds upon a strong foundation of existing research in machine learning, computer vision, and fog computing. By combining advanced techniques such as adaptive frame extraction, fog computing, and YOLO-based defect detection with a fusion model for predictive analytics, SeamSense sets a new standard for real-time quality monitoring in the apparel industry. The system not only improves the accuracy and speed of defect detection but also provides valuable insights for proactive quality control, reducing rework costs and enhancing production efficiency.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The primary challenge in the apparel manufacturing industry lies in maintaining consistent quality control while adhering to the demands of high-speed production. Traditional quality control methods, particularly manual inspections, are inherently flawed due to their labor-intensive nature, high dependency on human accuracy, and inability to provide real-time feedback. These methods often result in delayed detection of defects, leading to increased rework, production inefficiencies, and higher operational costs.

The apparel industry’s reliance on outdated inspection systems is particularly problematic when it comes to detecting seam defects, which require close attention and precision. Seam defects such as high-low errors, open seams, and stitch irregularities are often missed during manual inspections due to the complexity of the sewing process and varying environmental conditions, such as lighting and fabric movement. The lack of real-time detection further compounds the issue, as defects are only identified after production is complete, resulting in costly rework and reduced production quality.

SeamSense seeks to address these critical challenges by developing an advanced system for real-time seam defect detection. The research problem focuses on several key areas: the need for high-definition image capture that adapts to production conditions, real-time data processing that minimizes latency, and machine learning models capable of accurately identifying a wide range of defects. Moreover, SeamSense aims to integrate predictive analytics to anticipate future defects based on historical trends, thereby moving from a reactive to a proactive approach in quality control.

By automating defect detection and providing real-time feedback, SeamSense aims to revolutionize the quality control process, reducing the dependency on manual inspections and significantly improving production efficiency in the apparel industry.

RESEARCH GAP

The existing approaches to quality control in the apparel industry, particularly for seam defect detection, are fraught with limitations that hinder their effectiveness in fast-paced manufacturing environments. Traditional systems rely on static camera placements and full-frame image capturing, which often results in inefficient data processing and inconsistent defect detection. These systems also struggle to handle environmental variations, such as changing lighting conditions and machine vibrations, which can impact the quality of captured images.

Furthermore, current machine learning models used in defect detection tend to focus on a narrow range of defect types and lack the real-time performance needed for immediate corrective actions. Many existing systems also fail to integrate predictive analytics, limiting their ability to forecast future defects and implement proactive quality control measures.

SeamSense addresses these gaps by introducing a dynamic, real-time system that incorporates adaptive frame extraction, fog computing for edge-based image processing, and advanced YOLO models for seam defect detection. The system’s fusion model further enhances its capabilities by combining real-time data with historical trends, enabling predictive maintenance and reducing downtime. This comprehensive approach fills the critical gap in existing quality control methods, providing a more accurate, efficient, and proactive solution for seam defect detection in the apparel industry.

MAIN OBJECTIVE

The main objective of the SeamSense project is to develop an advanced real-time seam defect detection system for the apparel industry. This system aims to revolutionize quality control by leveraging machine learning algorithms, fog computing, and a fusion model for comprehensive defect analysis. SeamSense seeks to minimize manual intervention, reduce production delays, and enhance the overall quality of garment manufacturing.

SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
1. Defect Detection Using Machine Learning
One of the primary objectives of SeamSense is to accurately detect and classify seam defects using machine learning models, specifically CNNs and YOLO algorithms. The system is designed to identify defects such as open seams, high-low seams, and SPI (stitches per inch) errors in real-time. By training these models to handle diverse production conditions, SeamSense ensures high precision and reliability in defect detection, even in fast-moving environments.

2. Optimized Image Capture and Adaptive Frame Extraction
To ensure efficient and accurate defect detection, SeamSense employs an adaptive frame extraction technique that focuses on capturing high-quality images of garment seams. High-definition cameras are strategically placed near sewing machines to capture detailed images. The adaptive frame extraction system dynamically adjusts to environmental factors like lighting and machine vibrations, ensuring that only the most relevant frames are processed, thereby reducing computational load and improving system efficiency.

3. Real-Time Processing with Fog Computing
Fog computing plays a critical role in enabling real-time image processing in SeamSense. By performing initial image augmentation and content filtering at the edge, close to the source, fog computing reduces latency and optimizes bandwidth usage. This ensures that high-quality, relevant data is transmitted to the cloud for further analysis, allowing for faster and more accurate real-time defect detection.

4. Predictive Defect Analysis Using Fusion Modeling
SeamSense’s fusion model combines real-time defect detection with historical data analysis to predict potential future defects. By analyzing trends and patterns in defect occurrence, the fusion model provides insights into the underlying causes of defects, allowing manufacturers to implement proactive quality control measures. The model also incorporates worker-centric data, such as demographics and skill levels, ensuring a more tailored and accurate analysis of defect patterns.

In summary, SeamSense’s objectives encompass a holistic approach to quality control, integrating cutting-edge technologies to enhance defect detection, reduce production inefficiencies, and improve garment quality.

METHODOLOGY

The methodology behind SeamSense is designed to ensure the development of a robust, real-time quality monitoring system tailored for the apparel industry. It begins with requirement gathering and analysis, where both functional and non-functional requirements are identified to meet the specific needs of garment manufacturers. The system architecture revolves around the strategic placement of high-definition cameras near the sewing machines, capturing real-time video feeds of the garment seams.
